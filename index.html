01 about · ./background.md
read.me()

I came to software the long way. Seven years training as a behavior analyst before I shipped a single line of production code. I now do both — and I've come to think the most interesting software is the kind that respects how people actually work, not how they're supposed to.

That perspective shapes everything I build. AI workflows with human review. Dashboards that surface the next decision. Compliance tools designed around the moments people are most likely to skip a step. Mobile apps that respect attention rather than capture it.

As CTO of Enigma Software Systems, I lead product and engineering for AI tools that have to hold up to real audit, real scale, and real humans. Vault AI is the flagship — but the thinking shows up everywhere I touch.

Outside of Enigma I ship a lot. iOS apps, BCBA compliance tools, CRMs, dashboards. The throughline isn't a stack — it's that every project is a small experiment in making software that's used, not just installed.
